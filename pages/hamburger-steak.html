Hamburger Steak

Description

Recipe taken from: Just One Cookbook

Hamburger steak or hambagu, is a "steak" made from ground meat served typically with rice instead of buns. It is one of many popular yooshoku (Japanese style western food) dishes and can be served in a number of slight variations, from sautéed mushrooms and onions, with démi-glace sauce, or a fried egg.

Ingredients

  • onion
  • oil
  • ground beef & pork
  • panko
  • milk
  • red wine
  • egg
  • salt
  • pepper
  • nutmeg

Steps

  1. Chop the onion finely. Start by slicing the onion towards the root with 1/4 inch parallel cuts. Then cut perpendicular to the root again with 1/4 inch parallel cuts.
  2. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat and add the onion when ready. Sauté until tender and almost translucent.
  3. In a bowl, take the sautéed onions and add the ground beef and pork with the salt, pepper and nutmeg. Then add the milk, panko and egg.
  4. Mix the ingredients all together. With a silicone spatula. Switch to kneading the mixture with your hands until it is sticky and pale.
  5. Divide the hamburger mixture into 4-6 pieces. Take each piece to make a patty.
  6. With each patty, toss it from one hand to the other for about 5 times to release the air inside the mixture to prevent cracks while cooking. Then shape then into an oval.
  7. When you're ready to cook them, heat up oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  8. Cook the patties until they are brown on the bottom (~3 minutes) and flip over to cook until brown on the other side.
  9. Add red wine and reduce the heat to low heat. Cover with a lid and cook for 5-7 minutes in order to cook the inside of the patties.
