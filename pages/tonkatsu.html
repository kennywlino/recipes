Tonkatsu

Description

Tonkatsu is a popular traditional Japanese dish. It is made with pork and panko and is eaten with a special sauce. It is often served with thinly cut cabbage.

Ingredients

  • Pork; loin or fillet
  • Panko
  • Egg
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Neutral flavored oil
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Cabbage (optional)

Steps

  1. Pour the panko into a dish or pan so there is only a thin layer.
  2. Moisten the panko by using water in a spray bottle. Allow it to sit for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Shred the cabbage if using.
  4. Add slits to the connective tissue of the pork.
  5. Pound the meat until it's tender and reshape towards original shape.
  6. Create a mixture with the eggs and flour and a little bit of the oil.
  7. Take the pork and place it into the mixture of eggs/flour/oil. Make sure it is covered on all sides.
  8. Place the pork in the panko and cover it on all sides.
  9. Heat the oil over medium heat. Fry the tonkatsu when the temperature is around 340ÂºF.
  10. Fry the tonkatsu for 1 minute each on both sides.
  11. Remove and place on a wire rack until ready to serve.
